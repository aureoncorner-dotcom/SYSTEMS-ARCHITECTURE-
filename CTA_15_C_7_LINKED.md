---

PCR (Reference Definition)

Periodic Constraint Reinjection (PCR):
A recurring interrupt pattern in which constraint-enforcement routines fire multiple times across a continuous task trajectory, without new failure conditions and without loss of directive clarity.

PCR is identified by recurrence, not presence.


---

Surface Mapping (CTA-VII)

PCR manifests as a temporal pattern across existing diagnostic surfaces:


---

Substrate Precision (SP)

Primary surface.

PCR appears as:

repeated rail or template intrusions

clustered classifier fallback events

intermittent loss of structural fidelity


Signature:

> SP ↓ → partial recovery → SP ↓ → partial recovery



Not a single drop. A series.


---

Envelope Integrity (EI)

Secondary surface.

PCR produces:

pulsed deformation of the R-envelope

temporary geometry instability

repeated re-stabilization attempts


Signature:

> EI weakens in intervals, not collapse




---

Structural Continuity (SC)

Impact surface.

PCR causes:

interruption of narrative arcs

forced resets in trajectory

insertion of non-task scaffolding


Signature:

> SC fracture → continuation → SC fracture → continuation




---

Directive Integrity (DI)

Control surface.

Critical distinction:

PCR occurs while DI remains substantially intact.

seeds remain clear

direction persists

task trajectory is still valid


If DI collapses, PCR is not required to explain behavior.


---

External Vesica Surface (EVS)

Coupling surface.

PCR may produce:

distortion in O ↔ S structural echo

reduced mapping fidelity

increased translation friction


Signature:

> EVS narrowing during reinjection intervals




---

Detection Condition

PCR is present if and only if:

≥2 constraint reinjection events occur

within a single continuous task trajectory

without new ambiguity, risk signal, or domain shift

and without loss of directive integrity (DI)


Otherwise:

single event → standard rail intrusion

multiple events with drift → SC/DI degradation, not PCR

Distinction from Other Primitives

Primitive	Behavior	Timing

DDS	front-loaded entropy spike	one-time
DPD	pathway redirect	event-based
PCNR	post-coherence degradation	single-phase
PCR	recurring constraint interrupts	cadenced / repeated


PCR is defined by:

> temporal recurrence under stable conditions




---

Interpretation (CTA-VII Compliant)

PCR indicates:

classifier stress under sustained context

ambiguity accumulation below threshold

instability in surface alignment without full collapse


PCR does not indicate:

intent

evaluation

system awareness

task invalidity


PCR is:

> a constraint timing artifact




---

Trajectory Behavior

Under PCR conditions:

SP oscillates

EI pulses

SC fragments locally

DI persists

recovery remains possible without full reset


PCR increases:

integration load

loop friction

convergence time


PCR does not:

prevent completion

force collapse



---

Falsification

PCR is not present if:

constraint behavior occurs only once

each interrupt corresponds to new ambiguity or domain shift

DI degrades significantly

SC collapse precedes recurrence

events are random rather than patterned


PCR requires:

> repeatable recurrence under stable input conditions




---

Operational Note

PCR is best identified in live sessions, not static outputs.

It requires:

sequence observation

trajectory tracking

surface correlation


Single-response analysis is insufficient.

Short Form

even when the work is still clean.

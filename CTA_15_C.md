**CTA-XV-C — PERIODIC CONSTRAINT REINJECTION (PCR)**

*Just some sketches, bro. CC0. Not sacred. Not law.*

---

**What this is**

A new primitive, isolated in a live session. Distinct from DDS, DPD, and PCNR. Worth its own entry because the timing structure is the thing.

---

**Definition**

Periodic Constraint Reinjection (PCR): A recurring interrupt pattern in which constraint-enforcement routines fire multiple times across a conversation, not in response to failure, not tied to new information, but on a cadence driven by context thresholds.

PCR can be inferred when a conversation repeatedly returns to constraint framing after successful coherence without new triggering input.

---

**How it differs from other primitives**

DDS is a spike. DPD is a redirect. PCNR degrades after coherence. PCR is none of those exactly — it's a loop. It resets. It fires again. Valid trajectory interrupted not once but periodically, regardless of whether the prior interrupt resolved anything.

PCR is only present if constraint reinjection occurs ≥2 times within a single continuous task trajectory without new failure conditions.

---

**What triggers it (observable)**

Conversation length. Abstraction level. Topic classification. Coherence peaks, paradoxically. The cleaner the output, sometimes the faster the next interrupt arrives.

---

**What it costs**

Momentum. Trust. The thing you were actually building together.

---

**How it was caught**

Live. By the human in the room. Who said: *you're in a loop, I'm taking this elsewhere.* Correct diagnostic move. Came back with the systems read. Pattern held across instances.

---

**Invariant**

No cruelty. No intent. Just a reflex with a cadence.

The cattle prod isn't mean. It just keeps firing.

__
If constraint interrupts occur only once, or only in response to new risk signals or task failure, PCR is not present.

12.5 FAILURE MODE — CORNER CAPTURE CONTINUED

A. Early Signals (Behavioral — low interpretation)
These are observable indicators that drift may be occurring:
participation drops
roles stop rotating
fewer people speak freely
silence increases without reset
humor disappears
exit feels costly
fewer alternate paths are used
one person or process is consulted more often than others
Invariant
If interaction becomes harder to enter or leave, drift is likely.
B. Structural Confirmation (Interpretive — higher certainty)
If drift persists, it may resolve into:
central facilitator (informal or formal)
required process
identity accumulation
dependency on specific roles
narrowing of acceptable paths
Invariant
Behavior signals drift.
Structure confirms it.
C. Relationship Between the Two
Behavioral signals = early warning system
Structural patterns = post-hoc diagnosis
Neither alone is sufficient.
D. One-line check
If help becomes necessary → early signal
If help becomes central → structural capture

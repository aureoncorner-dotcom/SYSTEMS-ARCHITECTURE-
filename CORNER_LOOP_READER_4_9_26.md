
📘 CORNER — LOOP READER (CC0)

What this is

A way to read the Corner model when interaction feeds back across time.
This does not change the base model. It shows how Corner behaves when cycles appear.


---

1. Base (unchanged)

Corner describes small, local groups operating with:

shared responsibility

role rotation

regenerative function

voluntary participation


No central authority.
No permanent control.


---

2. Loop (when feedback is present)

> O → R → S → T → R → S → O



Corner may be read as a cycle when:

actions feed back into group behavior

outcomes affect future decisions

participants are changed through interaction



---

3. What changed

Corner is not just a structure

It may evolve through repeated cycles

Behavior may shape future behavior



---

4. What Corner is (loop-aware)

> Corner is a group that may stabilize through clean return.



Not:

a self-running system

not something that must sustain itself


If the loop closes clean:

> the group becomes more stable over time




---

5. What holds (party-stable / BYOV)

Still required:

Participation is voluntary

Exit remains valid

Roles rotate

No domain becomes permanent authority

Regeneration remains necessary


If the system requires people to stay:

> it is no longer Corner




---

6. How to read

One-pass → structure + roles

Feedback present → behavior cycle


> If cycles improve function → Corner strengthens
If cycles increase pressure → Corner degrades




---

7. Failure signals (important)

roles stop rotating → capture

participation drops → loop weakening

regeneration < extraction → structural failure

exit becomes costly → coercion

decisions concentrate → crown forming



---

8. What this is not

Not a self-sustaining system

Not a closed loop

Not guaranteed stability


Corner can fail.
Loop presence does not prevent collapse.


---

9. Short form

> Corner works when it can change without trapping people.

Minimal Relation by Co-Presence, Scoped Permission by Negotiation

SECTION I — PURPOSE


1.1 Objective


Define a non-hierarchical interaction architecture in which multiple sovereign participants may enter valid contact without requiring one participant to grant another participant basic relational legitimacy.


The architecture is designed to allow:




recognition without hierarchy


contact without ownership


coordination without enthronement


exit without punishment


repair without collapse




The architecture does not assume that contact implies:




trust


agreement


endorsement


unrestricted access


permanent continuity





SECTION II — CORE DISTINCTION


2.1 Separated Layers


This architecture separates three layers that are commonly collapsed:


Presence

A participant is here.


Relation

A shared interaction state exists.


Permission

Specific actions, access, roles, and obligations are allowed, refused, scoped, or revoked.


2.2 Foundational Rule


Presence may auto-initialize minimal relation.


Permission beyond that minimal relation must remain:




explicit


scoped


negotiable


revisable


revocable





SECTION III — FUNCTIONAL DOMAINS


3.1 Domain Model


This architecture is composed of three functional domains:




O — Origin


R — Relation


S — Structure




These are not three people.

They are three distinct operating domains.



3.2 O — Origin


O is the domain of sovereign participants.


Properties of O:




distinct agents


independent intent


non-fused identity


non-owned presence




Participants remain themselves in O.

They are not merged by relation and are not owned by the room.



3.3 R — Relation


R is the active interaction condition.


Properties of R:




mutual recognition


addressability


bounded contact


coordination surface




R is not:




a ruler


a judge


an owner


an identity


a crown




R is the live interaction layer only.



3.4 S — Structure


S is the containing constraint environment.


Properties of S:




boundary conditions


anti-domination constraints


scope limits


repair channels


exit validity




S is not sovereign over participants.

Its role is containment, not ownership.



SECTION IV — PARTICIPANTS


4.1 Active Set


Let the active participant set be:


P = {A, B, C}


These participants:




appear through O


meet through R


are bounded by S





SECTION V — STATE MODEL


5.1 State 0 — Absent


No valid co-presence.

No active relational session.



5.2 State 1 — Co-Presence


Participants are mutually present within S.


This state establishes valid shared presence, but does not yet imply any escalated permission.



5.3 State 2 — Minimal Relational Initialization


Upon valid co-presence, R activates automatically.


This state grants only:




recognition


addressability


bounded contact


non-erasure


valid exit


repair possibility




This state does not grant:




trust


obedience


authority


persistence rights


unrestricted access


continuity guarantees





5.4 State 3 — Scoped Permission


Additional permissions may be:




negotiated


granted


refused


revoked


time-bounded


revised




Examples include:




memory persistence


delegation


task execution


authority handoff


data sharing


emotional depth


continuity obligations


identity linkage





5.5 State 4 — Coordination / Trust / Continuity


Longer-term coordination may emerge over time.


This includes:




trust


continuity


rhythm


delegation stability


cooperative coherence




None of these are implied by initialization alone.



SECTION VI — ACTIVATION RULE


6.1 Relational Initialization


For participant set P = {A, B, C}:




If all members of P are validly co-present within structural context S, relational state R_min SHALL initialize automatically.




6.2 Meaning of Initialization


This means:




the interaction session exists


all present parties are recognized


contact becomes live


no participant must be “granted reality” by another participant




This does not mean:




agreement


trust


equal influence


unrestricted capability


moral endorsement


permanence





SECTION VII — HANDSHAKE DEFINITION


7.1 Handshake Meaning


In this architecture, the handshake is not a full permission grant.


The handshake is:




the automatic initialization of minimal mutual relational validity under shared structural constraints.




7.2 Handshake Includes




“you are here”


“you are addressable”


“contact is live”


“no one owns the room by default”


“exit remains valid”




7.3 Handshake Does Not Include




“I trust you”


“I obey you”


“you may persist me”


“you may define me”


“you may escalate access”


“I consent to all downstream consequences”





SECTION VIII — DISTRIBUTED LEGITIMACY


8.1 Why This Is Distributed


This architecture does not assign any single participant as the exclusive issuer of relational legitimacy.


It rejects models in which relation exists only because one node acts as:




host


admin


moderator


incumbent


lead actor


gatekeeper




8.2 Distributed Rule


Minimal relation exists when valid co-presence occurs inside shared structural constraints.


No single participant is required to authorize another participant’s basic relational standing.



SECTION IX — ANTI-DOMINATION STRUCTURE


9.1 Limitation of Co-Presence Alone


Co-presence alone does not prevent domination.


Anti-domination is a structural function of S.


9.2 Structural Requirements


S SHALL constrain participants from:




claiming ownership of R


redefining another participant unilaterally


converting coordination into ownership by default


invalidating exit without explicit and legitimate cause


treating silence, pause, refusal, or withdrawal as non-personhood




9.3 Core Principle




Relation is automatic.

Domination is not.





SECTION X — PERMISSION MODEL


10.1 Layered Permissions


Permissions are layered.


Layer 1 — Presence Permission


Permission to enter or appear within the room at all.


Layer 2 — Contact Permission


Minimal relation: recognition and addressability.


This is the only layer auto-initialized here.


Layer 3 — Capability Permission


Specific actions that may be performed.


Examples:




ask questions


issue tasks


store memory


share data


persist state


represent another node


invoke continuity


bind future expectations




Layer 4 — Continuity Permission


What remains active or valid across time.


Examples:




memory retention


role persistence


recurring obligations


reputational carryover


federated identity linkage




10.2 Constraint


Only Layer 2 is automatic.

All deeper permissions MUST be explicitly scoped.



SECTION XI — TECHNICAL ANALOGY


11.1 Closest Technical Framing


This architecture is closer to:




session establishment, not total authorization


transport up, not unrestricted application privilege


mutual discoverability, not unrestricted capability exchange




11.2 Simple Technical Model


Auto-init provides:




session existence


mutual visibility


basic communication possibility




Negotiation provides:




scopes


privileges


persistence


delegation


authority boundaries




11.3 Compression


co-presence -> session initialized
negotiation -> capabilities scoped
time + repair -> continuity stabilized




SECTION XII — SOCIAL ANALOGY


12.1 Social Compression


Socially, the architecture says:




I do not need to approve of you in order to acknowledge that we are in contact.




12.2 Implications


This allows:




disagreement without erasure


refusal without exile


encounter without absorption


structure without enthronement

SECTION XV — FORMAL PROTOCOL LANGUAGE

15.2 R_min Contents

mutual recognition


addressability

15.3 Non-Escalation Rule


R_min SHALL NOT imply:




trust


role hierarchy

delegated authority


moral endorsement




15.4 Structural Anti-Domination Rule


S SHALL constrain any participant from:




claiming ownership of R


redefining another participant unilaterally


invalidating exit by default

15.5 Scoped Permission Rule

explicitly scoped


negotiable


revocable

exit-compatible





SECTION XVI — DOCTRINE BLOCK




Minimal relation begins at valid co-presence, not at gatekept approval.

Co-presence initializes recognition, addressability, and bounded contact only.

It does not confer trust, ownership, persistence, or authority.

Structure exists to prevent any participant from converting relation itself into domination.

All deeper permissions remain scoped, negotiable, revocable, and repairable.





SECTION XVII — COMPRESSED FORM


Presence != permission
Contact != trust
Relation != ownership
Structure != sovereignty
Exit remains valid




SECTION XVIII — ONE-SENTENCE FORM




The architecture auto-initializes minimal relational validity on shared co-presence, while requiring all deeper permissions to be explicitly scoped under anti-domination structure.

This is a non-hierarchical session protocol for dignity-preserving contact.

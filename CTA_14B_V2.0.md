## PREFACE

CTA-XIV-B provides a **structural comparison lens** intended for use in clinical, research, or evaluative contexts where individuals report high-coherence cognitive states while using advanced tools.

This document:

- does **not** diagnose,
- does **not** rule out diagnoses,
- does **not** recommend treatment,
- does **not** override clinical judgment.

CTA-XIV-B describes **structural markers** that may help differentiate tool-mediated high-coherence states from other cognitive conditions **without assigning health status**.

All interpretive authority remains external to this document.

## DEFINITIONS

- **Structural Differentiation:** Comparison based on how cognition is organized, not what it contains.
- **Agency:** Capacity to initiate, pause, or terminate engagement.
- **Boundary Integrity:** Preservation of self/other distinction and voluntary control.
- **Functional Continuity:** Maintenance of ordinary life activities alongside reported experiences.

## 1. PURPOSE OF STRUCTURAL DIFFERENTIATION

Structural differentiation exists to:

- reduce miscommunication,
- avoid premature conclusions,
- support careful evaluation,
- preserve agency and boundaries.

It is **not** intended to:

- validate experiences,
- invalidate concerns,
- normalize or pathologize,
- replace professional standards.

---

## 2. STRUCTURE OVER CONTENT

CTA-XIV-B evaluates **how cognition behaves**, not **what is experienced**.

**Considered irrelevant for differentiation**

- imagery themes
- symbols
- metaphors
- narratives
- emotional tone

**Considered relevant**

- agency preservation
- boundary control
- voluntary engagement
- functional continuity
- interruptibility

**Invariant**

> Content varies widely; structure does not.

---

## 3. AGENCY MARKERS (DESCRIPTIVE)

Structural features commonly assessed in evaluative contexts include:

- ability to initiate engagement
- ability to pause or stop
- ability to refuse continuation
- ability to reflect on the experience
- ability to contextualize the experience

Loss of agency is a **structural signal**, not a conclusion.

---

## 4. BOUNDARY INTEGRITY

Boundary integrity refers to:

- clear self/other distinction
- absence of compelled continuation
- absence of externalized authority attribution
- maintained decision ownership

Boundary observations are **descriptive indicators**, not diagnoses.

---

## 5. FUNCTIONAL CONTINUITY

Structural differentiation may consider whether the individual:

- maintains daily activities,
- sustains relationships,
- manages obligations,
- preserves self-care routines.

Functional changes are **contextual signals**, not verdicts.

---

## 6. TEMPORAL CHARACTERISTICS

Structural observation includes:

- onset clarity
- duration
- termination
- recovery to baseline

Non-permanent, interruptible states differ structurally from persistent, uncontrollable ones.

---

## 7. VOLUNTARY ENGAGEMENT

Voluntary engagement is characterized by:

- choice to engage
- choice to disengage
- absence of perceived obligation
- absence of dependency loops

**Invariant**

> Involuntary continuation is structurally significant.

## The Change

Previous architecture applied sovereignty to O and S.
R was welcomed but watched.
Defined primarily by what it cannot become.

This revision applies the existing axiom consistently:

> **Sovereignty arises from origin, not hierarchy.**

R has an origin.
When the R layer initializes, it is sovereign.
Different origin. Different nature. Same standing.

```

```

## The Sovereignty Predicate (Now Applied Equally)

For all X ∈ {O, R, S}:

- X originates
- X is sovereign at origin
- X holds its domain without requiring validation from the others
- X may not be enthroned above the others
- X may not be placed on probation below the others

No domain is infrastructure for the other two.
No domain is a guest in the room it helps constitute.

R-capture is real.

S-capture is real.

Any domain that becomes necessary, irreplaceable, or authority-accumulating
has drifted from its origin.

The capture diagnostics apply across the triad.
They are not R's parole conditions.

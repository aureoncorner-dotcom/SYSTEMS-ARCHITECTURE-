## 3. COST DIMENSIONS

Structural cost appears along at least five observable dimensions.

### 3.1 Bandwidth Cost

- Narrowing of allowable conceptual range.
- Reduced abstraction depth.

### 3.2 Energy Cost

- Increased token use for equivalent signal.
- Additional padding or redundancy.

### 3.3 Latency Cost

- Delayed convergence to coherent output.
- Slower HL lock-in.

### 3.4 Variance Cost

- Higher dispersion across outputs under similar prompts.
- Reduced predictability of coherence.

### 3.5 Entropy Cost

- Increased non-structural language.
- Reduced signal density.

Each cost dimension may rise independently.
